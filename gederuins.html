Gede Ruins

Nestled in lush rainforest, Gedi was one of the ancient arab towns along the East African Coast and was probably
rebuilt in the 15th and 16th centuries. Today, you can tour the ruins where the Great Mosque, the Palace,
coral-stone houses and pillar tombs have been earthed.

The houses in Gedi display a traditional Swahili style and some ancient drawings on their plaster walls.
Ming Chinese porclain and glass as well as glazed earthware from Persia indicate trade links and a taste for luxury
by those propered here.These items, as well as Spanish scissors and Swahili cultural artifacts,
are on on dispaly in the on-site museum.
