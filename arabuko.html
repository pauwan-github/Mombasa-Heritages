Arabuko Sokoke Forest

The Arabuko Sokoke Forest is East Africa's largest remaining indigenous coastal forest. The forest plays host to plenty or rare species,including birds, butterflies, ampbibiaNS AND PLANTS.

More than 260 species of birds are found here, including several threatened species such as the spotted ground thrush and Clarke's weaver.

Rare mammals that call the Arabuko Sokoke Forest home are the golden rumped elephant shrew, bushy-tailed mongoose and Ader's dulker. The forest is a favorite picnic spot for residents and visitors.
